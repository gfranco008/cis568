Phase Plane: Vector Field and Nullclines

Arrows show the flow direction at each point in the phase plane, colored by speed (dark = slow, bright = fast). The cyan line is the x-nullcline (dx/dt = y′ = 0). The orange S-curve is the y-nullcline (dy′/dt = 0, i.e. y′ = x / μ(1−x²)). Their only intersection is the origin — an unstable equilibrium marked by the white circle. The dashed curve is the limit cycle. Drag μ to see how all three elements reshape together.

Research significance: Nullcline analysis is the primary geometric tool for understanding 2D ODEs without solving them analytically. The limit cycle's existence is topologically guaranteed by the Poincaré–Bendixson theorem whenever an unstable focus is enclosed by a bounded trapping region. This visualization makes that argument visually concrete and shows how the phase-plane geometry — and therefore the system's qualitative behavior — changes with μ.
