Time Evolution from an Initial Condition

Starting from x(0) = 0.5, x′(0) = 0 — a point inside the limit cycle — the trajectory spirals outward and gradually settles onto the closed limit cycle. The faint dashed curve shows the reference limit cycle. Changing μ restarts the simulation and reshapes the attractor; the time scrubber lets you pause at any moment.

Research significance: Transient dynamics reveal how quickly a system converges to steady state. For small μ convergence is slow (weak effective damping); for large μ it is rapid. Characterizing this timescale is essential for ODE solver validation and for understanding how fast a parametric system "forgets" its initial conditions — a key question in stability and sensitivity analysis.
