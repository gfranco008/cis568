Research Context: Why Chebyshev + Barycentric?

This page interpolates the Van der Pol trajectory x(t) itself, not a toy function. The gray curve is a high-resolution RK4 reference solution on a post-transient time window, and both colored curves are degree-n barycentric interpolants built from sampled values of that same trajectory.

Equispaced nodes tend to amplify boundary oscillations at higher n (Runge-type behavior), while Chebyshev-Lobatto nodes cluster near interval endpoints and typically reduce the worst-case interpolation error. Barycentric evaluation avoids explicitly solving Vandermonde systems, so it is the standard stable implementation for high-order interpolation.

Research significance: the convergence plot gives direct evidence of how node choice affects error decay as n increases for your Van der Pol data. This supports method selection when building polynomial surrogates used in downstream analysis, parameter studies, or reduced-order modeling workflows.
