The Limit Cycle as a Global Attractor

Eight trajectories are launched from very different starting points — four inside the limit cycle (spiraling outward) and four outside (spiraling inward). Dashed circles mark each initial position; colored dots track the current location in real time. Despite the radically different starting points, all eight paths converge to the same closed curve.

Research significance: The Van der Pol limit cycle is a global attractor — every trajectory except the unstable equilibrium at the origin is drawn to it, regardless of initial energy or position. This robustness distinguishes nonlinear self-sustained oscillators from conservative systems where motion depends entirely on initial conditions. Demonstrating global attraction numerically supports theoretical claims about solution robustness across parameter regimes.
